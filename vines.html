Иван Федорович Карамазов (Ванка)

Ivan Karamazov

Vines

“I accept God and I am glad to, what's more I accept His wisdom, His purpose....Yet would you believe it, in the final result I don’t accept this world of Gods and although I know it exists, I don’t accept it at all” (218)
“His mind is in bondage. He is haunted by a great unresolved doubt. He is one of those who don’t want millions, but an answer to their questions” (82)

As the vine stretches and envelopes over all obstacles searching for the light with roots a couple inches deep in many places, so Ivan has spent the latter of his life desperately combing the universe for the truth and thus his confidence and firm statements are a guise to hide a plague of uncertainty. Ivan admits to Alyosha that he “accepts God” but he cannot “accept this world of God’s” especially because of the suffering of children (218). He has planted himself shallowly into christianity and atheism, faith and logic, and continues to seek vital soil to attempt to plant himself firmly. Alyosha sees Ivan for what he truly is and defends him to Mitya when Mitya suggests he is devilish and would sell out the family: “He is haunted by a great unresolved doubt. He is one of those who don’t want millions, but an answer to their questions” (82). Ivan desires the truth more than any other conceivable thing and yet he himself imprisoned his soul in reason so that he is unable to reach the light. The suffering aflicting his soul is from knowing all but lacking the ability to discern the truth.
