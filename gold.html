Иван Федорович Карамазов (Ванка)

Ivan Karamazov

Gold

“I don't want to corrupt you or turn you from your stronghold, perhaps I want to be healed by you” (219)
“It was only with you, with your help, I killed him...You said ‘everything was lawful’ and how frightened you are now” (568)

Ivan’s spirit is not unmoving and rigid but on the contrary, like gold, he is malleable and susceptible to strong emotions. Not only is he able to change, Ivan is desperate for novel ideas and interpretations that can strengthen his constitution similar to how gold is made strong by combining it with other metals. After thoroughly questioning the belief in God to Alyosha, Ivan sighs that his intentions are not to pry Alyosha from his God. Ivan hopes he might be “healed” by Alyosha’s faith (219). This reveals his great passion for truth and unrelenting need to fully understand; Ivan hoped Alyosha had the key to unlock a reasoning for God. In his own paper, Ivan argued for the absolute necessity of God in history and that faith in immortality drove society forward, but he is left helpless to reconcile these facts with true proof of God. Thus he calls himself an atheist because he cannot attain faith while chained to his own intellect. His words and philosophies return to him bloodied after his father’s murder. Smerdyakov, the murderer and servant, insists he killed Fyodor “with your help” blaming his actions on Ivan’s notion that “everything is lawful” because there is no God (568). The accusation wreaks Ivan’s soul and his health declines as he realizes how his seemingly innocuous ideas urged a younger mind to rationalize such a horrific crime. His pliable soul takes the guilt as a destructive punch because he recognizes that he did have contempt for his father, and his need to find the faith to strengthen himself becomes paramount.
